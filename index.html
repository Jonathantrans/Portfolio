Mask or Die

Live Demo · Devpost Website

Mask or Die is a game development project submitted to SacHacks, a Sacramento Hackathon where there is a time constraint of 48 hours to design and code a project. My team and I utilized C# and Unity to code the mechanics of the game, creating a first-person, mask-shooting game as a non-profit PPE for the Sacramento community in order to raise awareness of COVID-19 cases.

The example shown above displays how Google Maps SDK was used to import the map of Sacramento, California, focusing on the environment around and with the capital building. As the player walks around in the game, the Google Maps SDK will build the streets of Sacramento around you, looking exactly as it does in the 3D view of Google Maps.

PYR Resource Pack

Live Demo · YouTube Channel

PYR is a resource/texture pack add-on for Minecraft and is one of my first "serious" endeavors, created in early 2016. While undoubtedly a passion project, this taught me many practical lessons while developing it. I had to look at Minecraft's source code to find out how to utilize certain lightmaps for sky textures and lighting angles from objects such as torches, the sun, and the moon, one of my first experiences in building from an existing codebase and designing textures from scratch. As one of my first projects to gain public traction, I had to respond to user feedback and deal with bug reports, giving me a taste of maintaining a project. I couldn't have done this without my fans supporting me throughout my years on YouTube, receiving 600,000+ views total.

PYR is hosted on MediaFire, where it currently has over 150,000 downloads.

Creating Chess

GitHub Respository

Chess has always intrigued me when I was a child. If Chess has taught me one thing, it is that persistence pays off in the end. After gaining experience with technical skills such as C++ and the use of the SFML interface, I was able to program a virtual game of Chess. All the functionalities and pieces run according to how the rules are in the game. I would love to make improvements to the AI and give it more capabilities, using algorithms that can make the enemy’s pawns more strategic and challenging.

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a chessboard (a checkered game board) with 64 squares arranged in an 8x8 grid where each player will begin with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. Each of the six types has its own unique moves. The image on the left demonstrates the White Queen’s ability to move diagonally, vertically, and horizontally. The image on the right demonstrates the White Pawn moving to G8, reaching the enemy’s side, and declaring a new pawn choice.
